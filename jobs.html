Open Position

AI-Assisted Software Quality Engineer

Remote Full-Time Engineering Posted May 2026

BIITS LLC is growing its engineering team and looking for a careful, systematic collaborator who is fluent with everyday computers and curious about AI-assisted workflows. The work is structured, well-specified, and designed to be approachable: if you can follow precise instructions, operate a modern browser and desktop environment, and describe what you see with accuracy, you can contribute meaningfully on day one. No prior software-industry résumé is expected.

About the Role

As an AI-Assisted Software Quality Engineer at BIITS LLC, you will execute defined test plans, exercise applications through the same interfaces our clients use, and record outcomes in plain, traceable language. You will work alongside a small engineering team so that releases ship with predictable behavior and regressions are caught before they reach production.

You will routinely use AI tools — conversational assistants, guided test suggestions, and similar surfaces — as part of a supervised workflow: the emphasis is on disciplined judgment, not on memorizing frameworks. We care that you can steer the tools, cross-check their output, and stop when something does not match the specification.

Key Responsibilities
  • Execute written test scripts and checklists against web applications and documented API behaviors, confirming each step against expected outcomes.
  • Use AI-assisted tools (for example, conversational coding or testing assistants) under team guidance to draft or refine test ideas, then validate results manually.
  • Operate automated test runners when provided — launching suites, reading pass/fail output, and capturing logs or screenshots for handoff.
  • Perform structured walkthroughs of new features: linear paths, boundary inputs, and deliberate retries to surface inconsistent states.
  • Open, update, and resolve tickets that describe defects with reproducible steps, environmental notes, and unambiguous language.
  • Attend brief async or live checkpoints with developers to confirm whether an observation is a defect, a specification ambiguity, or intended behavior.
  • Sanity-check AI-generated or AI-augmented product behavior against written acceptance criteria, including obvious failure modes (empty states, timeouts, malformed input).
  • Keep internal runbooks and test notes legible so the next person can repeat your sequence without interpretation.
  • When requested, witness release demonstrations and record a concise sign-off checklist.
  • Flag tooling friction or unclear instructions so the team can tighten the process — incremental refinement is part of the role.
Required Qualifications
  • Operational fluency with contemporary desktop and browser-based computing: multi-tab research, downloads, bookmarks, and password hygiene without frequent procedural support.
  • Instruction fidelity — you can translate written specifications, annotated screenshots, and stepwise procedures into exact sequences of clicks and keystrokes, and you notice when a step cannot be completed as written.
  • Discriminating observation — you attend to typography, layout shifts, error strings, loading states, and subtle copy differences the way a careful editor reads galley proofs.
  • Composed written articulation in English: complete sentences, neutral tone, and reproducible steps so another person can follow your account without guessing your intent.
  • Structured digital hygiene — organized folders, consistent file naming, and the habit of verifying URLs, accounts, and environment indicators before concluding a task.
  • Measured engagement with AI interfaces — you can issue bounded prompts, compare outputs against a source of truth, and resist treating probabilistic text as authoritative without cross-check.
  • Async discipline in a remote setting: responsive messaging, predictable availability windows, and the patience to wait for clarifications instead of improvising silently.
Preferred Qualifications
  • Intellectual curiosity about how large language models behave when prompts edge toward ambiguity or contradiction.
  • Comfort reading lightly technical prose (release notes, JSON fragments in documentation, simple HTTP status explanations) even if you do not author that material yourself.
  • Familiarity with collaborative surfaces such as shared documents, comment threads, or basic issue trackers — school projects and personal use count.
  • Keyboard fluency and low-friction typing; you are not slowed down when asked to transcribe observations in real time.
  • An eye for inclusive design basics — contrast legibility, focus order, and whether controls remain operable without a mouse.
  • Calm when context-switching between a small number of parallel assignments, each with its own checklist.
What We Offer
  • Fully remote position with flexible scheduling built around outcomes, not hours.
  • Direct access to leadership — your work and ideas will be seen and acted on.
  • A fast-moving environment where you will work on a variety of real-world client software projects.
  • Opportunity to help refine QA checklists and habits as the firm grows.
  • Competitive compensation.
